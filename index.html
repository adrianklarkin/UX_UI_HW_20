Fillaheart4kids.org Web Redesign

role/team

I was one of three UX Designners. We gathered data to figure out the best approach to get people to donate more often once they were on the website.

Tools

Figma, Miro, Invision, Adobe XD

Duration

3 weeks

The Challenge

After talking with the stakeholders, what they wanted to improveo upon was notation clicks. We noticed they were getting more traffic on their Facebook page than their actual website. We needed to get people on the website and guide a protential donor to the “donate” button. I needed to find out who would typically be willing to donate first and why.

Problem Statement

How might we improve the FillAHeart4kids website to help educators who have several students who are homeless or in foster care feel more confident about making a donation to their organization online?

Goals

Update website’s layout and navigation design while still keeping the integrity of the orignal website’s feel creating less clicks and guiding the user to the “donate” button.

Conduct research, interviews and gather insights on why, when and who typically would donate to this cause. Getting to know who our user is and how they feel can help us design a better experience.

Goal Three

Research & Analysis

Soon after our meeting with the stakeholder, we carried out my user interviews. These interviews gave us the data and insight we needed in order to successfully redesign a website with a better user experience. During these interviews, we found out the user’s navigation habits, preferences and what tools they gravitate towards when using websites.

User Persona

The proto persona, we came up with, combined with the user interviews and empathy map, lead us to create this user persona. Now that we have an Idea of the donating user, we can move forward with specifically identifying the problem which should reflect my redesign.

Concepts, Sketcing and Wireframes

Remembering the original design, you can already see a more structured layout for the users who wanted easier navigation. During the user testing phase we discovered the user wanted to see more information imbedded into the “learn more” experience. Also, even though we had brought a cleaner design to the website, users were still confused on how to donate to a SPECIFIC cause.

Visiual Design & Prototyping

During the last user tests I discovered that the users weren’t quite aware of some key navigation sequences. A few additional texts and arrow location lead the user to use the donating screen better. Also changing the gradient on the header and darkening the “donate” button had a better response. Switching the text from black to white gave the overall look a needed pop.

High Fedelity Prototyping

So as I mention, we kept the color scheme from the original design but gave it a more modern layout. The mission is right upfront when the home page loads and the user get a clearer look at what this website stands for.

Challenges and Conclusion

As technology gets more and more automated and contact with humans become more limited, the feeling to stay connected is actually still there and in some cases even more so. Each case study I do, I get better at the process. I’ve realized I really need to crush the interviews and testing. That is the bread and butter of this whole thing. With the right questions, one could find a hidden need that can help everyone. Listening and taking criticism is also key to great design. Asking the right questions, listening and humility added together equals great design.
